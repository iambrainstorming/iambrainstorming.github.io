01
Raise Minimum Wage for Every Worker

Every worker — IT professional, factory worker, delivery worker, or daily labourer — deserves dignified pay.

Daily Labourers
₹30,000
per month minimum
Graduates & Skilled
₹40,000
per month minimum

Workers are not machines. Higher wages increase purchasing power, keep money circulating in the economy, and boost demand for local businesses. An economy grows when ordinary people can afford to spend — not when wealth concentrates at the top.

National Job Registry — All companies must list job openings on one public platform: no middlemen, no scams, no hidden recruitment systems.
02
Universal Basic Income (UBI)
Phase 1 — Launch
₹5,000
Women & unemployed citizens
Phase 2 — Expanded
₹10,000
Gradual increase to all

UBI gives people survival security so they can take risks, learn skills, start businesses, or escape exploitative jobs. It reduces desperation and creates entrepreneurship.

Today's larger economic problem is weak demand and low purchasing power among ordinary citizens. Modern technology means society rarely faces pure supply-side shortages. The goal is efficient, sustainable, and citizen-driven growth.

03
Remote Work Rights

Make remote work compulsory wherever physically possible. Millions waste hours in traffic and overcrowded cities daily. Remote work saves fuel, reduces pollution, improves mental health, and gives workers flexibility and family time.

  • Save youth from forced migration to expensive metro cities
  • Reduce fuel consumption and urban pollution
  • Help smaller towns grow economically
  • Give workers more flexibility and family time
04
Six-Hour Work Day
Standard Work Day
6 hrs
Down from 8–10 hrs

Human productivity does not increase endlessly with longer hours. Overwork destroys mental health, creativity, and family life. A shorter work day can improve productivity, employment, and quality of life.

05
Approval Voting Reform

Implement approval voting in elections. Citizens should be able to support multiple acceptable candidates instead of being trapped into choosing only one. Approval voting reduces hyper-partisanship and encourages consensus politics.

06
100% VVPAT Counting

Mandatory 100% verification of VVPAT slips. Democracy depends on trust. Every vote must be transparently auditable to protect confidence in elections.

07
Structural Reform of Examination System

India's exam system rewards memorisation and is vulnerable to paper leaks. Education should test understanding, reasoning, creativity, and problem-solving.

  • Large national question banks with questions promoting critical thinking
  • Randomised selection of questions for each exam
  • Question papers printed only 3–5 hours before exams
  • High-security decentralised printing centres
08
Non-Partisan Movement

Anyone who supports this manifesto is welcome — BJP, Congress, Left, Right, or independent. The movement is issue-based, not personality-based. Citizens matter more than party labels.

09
Protect Voting Rights

No deletion of legitimate voters from electoral rolls. Strict action against ECI officials who illegally remove citizens' voting rights. Voting is a fundamental democratic right.

10
50% Reservation for Women
  • 50% reservation for women in Parliament
  • 50% reservation in all cabinet positions
  • No waiting for delimitation exercises

Women are half the population. Representation should reflect reality, not tokenism.

11
No Post-Retirement Political Rewards for Chief Justices

No Chief Justice should receive Rajya Sabha seats or political rewards after retirement. Judicial independence must remain unquestionable. Courts should never appear politically influenced.

12
Reduce Political Hostility

With approval voting, punitive anti-defection measures become less necessary because politics becomes less polarised. The goal is cooperation, not permanent political warfare.

13
Release Protestors

Free all protestors jailed for dissent against the government. Democracy cannot survive if criticism is treated as a crime. Peaceful dissent is a democratic right.

14
Decentralized and Open Media

Information power should not belong to a handful of corporations or politically connected billionaires.

  • Fair access to TV broadcasting for independent and community media
  • Boycott disinformation-driven media monopolies
  • Support open-source, decentralised online media platforms
  • Community-driven communication systems respecting freedom of speech
15
15-Minute Car-Free Cities

Build cities focused on walking, e-cycles, public transport, and pedestrian lanes. Cities should be designed for humans, not traffic congestion. Car-free urban planning improves health, reduces pollution, and makes cities more livable.

THE COCKROACH SURVIVES

The new manifesto of the Cockroach Janta Party stands for ordinary citizens who continue surviving despite unemployment, corruption, economic pressure, rising living costs, and political neglect.

The "cockroach" symbolises resilience — people who continue struggling, adapting, and surviving even when the system ignores them.

This manifesto is not about blind outrage or empty slogans. It is a real test for Gen Z and millennials — whether they can bend the system through perseverance, organisation, advocacy, and democratic pressure.
