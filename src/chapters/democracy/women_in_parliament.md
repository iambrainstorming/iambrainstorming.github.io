# Country Will Remain Safe When Women Lead the Nation

Date: 13-04-2026
Update: 19-04-2026



The future of a nation is shaped not just by policies, but by the values of those who govern it. A country becomes truly stable, inclusive, and forward-looking when leadership reflects the lived realities of its people. In that sense, empowering women in leadership is not just a matter of fairness—it is a matter of national strength and safety.

The recent move by the Union Cabinet to fast-track women’s reservation—implementing 33% reservation in the Lok Sabha and State Assemblies starting from the 2029 general elections—is a significant step. With the proposed expansion of Lok Sabha seats from 543 to 816, around 273 seats will be reserved for women, including proportional representation for Scheduled Castes (SC) and Scheduled Tribes (ST). This builds upon the **Nari Shakti Vandan Adhiniyam (Constitution 106th Amendment Act, 2023)**.

But the question remains: is 33% enough?

---

## Women Leaders Are Proven to Be More Effective

Evidence across the world consistently shows that women are not just equally capable leaders—they are often more effective.

The *Ready-Now Leaders* report by The Conference Board found that organizations with at least 30% women in leadership roles are **12 times more likely** to be among the top 20% in financial performance.

Similarly, research from The Leadership Circle—based on data from over 84,000 leaders and 1.5 million evaluators—shows that women outperform men across **every level of leadership** and across all age groups.

This is not a marginal difference. It is systemic.

[New Research: Women More Effective Than Men In All Leadership Measures](https://www.forbes.com/sites/kevinkruse/2023/03/31/new-research-women-more-effective-than-men-in-all-leadership-measures/)

---

## Women Bring Empathy—A Missing Element in Governance

One of the strongest differentiators in leadership is empathy—and women consistently score higher.

Studies across 57 countries involving over 300,000 participants show that women demonstrate significantly higher **cognitive empathy**, meaning the ability to understand and respond to others’ emotions.

Key insights:

* Women perform better in tests like the *Reading the Mind in the Eyes*, which measures emotional intelligence.
* In real-world situations, women are more likely to intervene and help in distress scenarios.
* Brain studies show higher activation in regions linked to emotional processing.
* Social expectations also amplify this difference, as empathy is both expressed and reinforced culturally.

Empathy in leadership translates into:

* Better welfare policies
* More inclusive decision-making
* Reduced conflict and polarization

A nation led with empathy is a nation that listens before it reacts—and that alone improves internal stability.

[Females perform better than males on a ‘theory of mind’ test across 57 countries](https://www.cam.ac.uk/research/news/females-perform-better-than-males-on-a-theory-of-mind-test-across-57-countries)

## Why 50% Reservation Is the Real “Fair Share”

Women constitute nearly **50% of the population**. A 33% reservation, while historic, still falls short of true representation.

If democracy means equal voice, then **50% reservation is not radical—it is rational**.

This is not about charity or symbolic inclusion. It is about correcting structural imbalance and ensuring governance reflects the population it serves.

---

## Women Voters Already Decide Elections

Political reality already acknowledges women’s importance.

Women voters now form nearly **half of the electorate**, and their voting patterns often decide elections. This is why:

* Direct cash transfer schemes targeting women have proven electorally successful
* Welfare policies increasingly focus on women as primary beneficiaries

Women are not just participants—they are **the core political force**. Representation in leadership must catch up with this reality.

---

## Women Leadership Leads to Economic Growth

Beyond ethics and representation, there is a clear economic case.

Research highlighted by World Economic Forum shows that:

* A **10 percentage point increase** in women’s parliamentary representation correlates with a **0.7% increase in GDP growth**

Why?
Because women leaders tend to prioritize:

* Childcare infrastructure
* Paid family leave
* Pay transparency
* Education and health

These policies increase workforce participation, improve productivity, and create long-term economic stability.

[Countries with more women in government are more prosperous](https://www.weforum.org/stories/2023/11/women-government-representation/)

## A Different Vision of Power

Those who understand the responsibility of nurturing life bring a fundamentally different perspective to governance.

Women leaders often prioritize:

* Sustainability over short-term gain
* Community welfare over individual power
* Stability over aggression

This is not about saying women are inherently “better” humans—it is about recognizing that **diverse lived experiences create better decisions**.

A system dominated by one perspective becomes fragile. A system that includes women becomes resilient.

## Why Wait for Delimitation? Implement 33% Now

Linking women’s reservation to delimitation creates an unnecessary and unfair delay.

The core issue is this: **delimitation based on population growth risks rewarding states with higher population increases while penalizing those that invested in development, education, and family planning.**

States that successfully controlled population growth—while improving GDP, literacy, and overall human development—stand to gain only a marginal increase in seats (often just 3–10 seats). In contrast, states with higher population growth and weaker development indicators could see their representation nearly double.

This creates a deeply inequitable outcome:

* It **disincentivizes responsible governance**
* It **penalizes long-term development policies**
* It **distorts fair political representation**

Yes, increasing total seats is necessary to improve representation in a growing democracy. But **how those seats are distributed matters just as much as how many are added**.

A fair approach could be:

* Increase seats **proportionally across all states (e.g., 50% increase for every state)**, ensuring balance
  **or**
* Implement **33% women’s reservation immediately using the current seat structure**, without waiting for delimitation

Delaying implementation until after delimitation effectively **pushes women’s representation further into the future**, turning a constitutional promise into a postponed reality.

The Nari Shakti Vandan Adhiniyam is meant to empower women—not delay their participation.

Every election without adequate representation is a lost opportunity.

If the intent is genuine, **there is no structural barrier to implementing 33% reservation in the very next election**.

Delaying it is not a technical necessity—it is a political choice.

## Conclusion: Safety Through Inclusion

A country is safest not when it is controlled, but when it is balanced.

Women’s leadership brings:

* Empathy in decision-making
* Stability in governance
* Inclusiveness in policy
* Long-term economic growth

The 33% reservation is a beginning—but not the destination.

If India truly wants to build a strong, stable, and future-ready nation, it must move toward **equal representation**.

Because a nation led by women is not just more just—it is more secure, more humane, and more prepared for the future.
