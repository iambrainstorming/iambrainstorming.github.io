A lot of things happened during the covid pandemic. There were unprecedented restrictions on travel and work.

  
[Here is an excerpt from BBC:](https://www.bbc.com/future/article/20210312-covid-19-paused-climate-emissions-but-theyre-rising-again)  
"By May 2020, a landmark study combined government lockdown policies and activity data from around the world to predict as much as a 7% fall in CO2 emissions by the end of the year, a figure later confirmed by the Global Carbon Project"

Covid-19 has fundamentally shifted the way we work. People no more have to commute to the office and can save hours. [It was a way to tackle the worst job culture of India.](https://iambrainstorming.wordpress.com/2020/02/16/how-to-beat-the-worst-job-culture-of-india/)  

How students learn was turned upside down. It turned hybrid. They attend online classes of school and go for private tuitions for learning. Children are no longer in a rat race as there were no offline exams. Only low stake online exams where you have the freedom to submit your answers at your time. Children got sufficient time to explore new things.

Yes, covid 19 has enormous challenges, but these were for good. [We could start better structural changes](https://technoperiod.blogspot.com/2018/10/the-competitive-collaboration-algorithm.html) as old problematic structures were smashed up by covid.

Take, for example, I started Avrit learning at the end of this year to teach children critical thinking where you are not bound to the school curriculum. Children had sufficient time to study.  
[https://avrit.reaudito.com/](https://avrit.reaudito.com/)

> But something different happened today. Schools have opened. The reply of children is they have no time that broke my heart.

Some of the parent associations are trying to delay the school opening. Many students are fighting for online mode of exams. But these won't work if the government wants to go back to previous days of hopelessness.

We are guided by fictional rules of govt. Humans are social animals. People who deny obeying fear being ostracized by the crowd.

Evil has returned. Welcome to 2022.
